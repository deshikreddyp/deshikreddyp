Mixed-dimensional fluid-structure interaction model for simulating CSF flow in the spinal canal

Publication: Putluru, D.R., Tepole, A.B. & Gomez, H. Mixed-dimensional fluid–structure interaction simulations reveal key mechanisms of cerebrospinal fluid dynamics in the spinal canal. Fluids and Barriers of the CNS 22, 81 (2025). DOI link

Cerebrospinal fluid flow dynamics plays a critical role in structural disorders of the central nervous system and in the design of effective procedures for intrathecal drug delivery. Medical imaging has only partially characterized CSF dynamics, while computational models can provide a high-resolution description and improve mechanistic understanding.

Many anatomically accurate computational models of CSF dynamics in the spinal canal ignore tissue compliance, even though compliance is important for capturing pulse wave velocity and the craniocaudal decay of CSF pulsations.

This project proposes a mixed-dimensional fluid–structure interaction method for high-fidelity simulation of CSF dynamics in anatomically accurate spinal canal models while accounting for compliance effects from the dura mater and epidural fat. The approach bypasses a computational bottleneck associated with the multiscale geometry of spinal tissues.

The results show that accurate modeling of tissue compliance is critical for capturing key aspects of CSF dynamics and opens new possibilities for optimizing intrathecal drug delivery and understanding structural abnormalities of the CNS.

Numerical and experimental analysis of a Savonius-style wind turbine operating at low wind speeds

The performance of a Savonius-style wind turbine depends on overlap ratio and blade shape characteristics. This project aimed to optimize geometric parameters of the blade design, including aspect ratios, blade profile, and overlap distance, to improve the power conversion efficiency parameter coefficient of moment Cm.

Modeling of film boiling around a hot sphere in still fluid using OpenFOAM

A hot sphere of diameter 0.00635 m is placed at the center of a water pool of dimensions 0.2 m × 0.2 m. The sphere temperature is assumed to be 1063 K and constant in time. The full problem is modeled in OpenFOAM and validated using the Nusselt number and heat transfer coefficient.

Numerical simulation of shockwave passing through a Y-Split

This project was completed as part of a Compressible Flow course at IIT Roorkee. The objective was to model shock-wave passage through a Y-split channel using OpenFOAM v6.0. The rhoCentralFoam solver was used for its shock-capturing capability. The work supports the design of tunnels with reduced damage in blast scenarios. A parametric analysis was performed by varying the bifurcation angle to study the resulting destructive effects.

1D FEM code [Open Source]

As part of the MIN-324 Finite Element Method course at IIT Roorkee, I studied strong and weak forms, residual methods, Galerkin methods, Ritz methods, and 1D/2D finite element frameworks for solid mechanics problems. I then developed a 1D FEM code that can solve a broad class of problems.
