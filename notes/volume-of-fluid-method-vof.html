Volume of Fluid (VOF) Method

Category: CFD · Multiphase Flow · Interface Tracking · VOF

Last updated: ...

The Volume of Fluid (VOF) method is a numerical technique for tracking and locating the free surface (or fluid-fluid interface) in multiphase flow simulations. It belongs to the class of Eulerian interface-capturing methods.

The Scalar Function ($\alpha$).

A scalar field $\alpha$ (volume fraction) is defined in each cell:

  • $\alpha = 1$: Cell is full of fluid 1.
  • $\alpha = 0$: Cell is full of fluid 2.
  • $0 < \alpha < 1$: Cell contains the interface.

Governing Equations

The transport of the interface is governed by a passive advection equation:

\[ \frac{\partial \alpha}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\mathbf{u} \alpha) = 0 \]

In the VOF method, a single set of momentum equations is shared by the fluids. The physical properties (density and viscosity) are determined by the volume fraction:

\[ \rho = \alpha \rho_1 + (1-\alpha) \rho_2 \] \[ \mu = \alpha \mu_1 + (1-\alpha) \mu_2 \]
Numerical Diffusion.

Standard advection schemes tend to smear the interface over several cells. To maintain a sharp interface, specialized schemes like MULES (Multidimensional Universal Limiter with Explicit Solution) or Geometric Reconstruction (PLIC) are required.

Surface Tension.

Surface tension forces are typically modeled using the Continuum Surface Force (CSF) model, where the surface force is converted into a volumetric body force acting on cells in the interface region.
