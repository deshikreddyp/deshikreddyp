Boundary Layer Problem

💡 The complexity of convective heat transfer comes from nonlinearity in the Navier-Stokes equations and coupling between velocity and temperature fields. The boundary-layer idea (Prandtl, 1904) simplifies the equations by neglecting terms that are asymptotically small in thin-layer regions.

  1. Write down the full governing equations and boundary conditions.
  2. Simplify the equations using physically justified assumptions.
    1. Physical reasoning and modeling assumptions.
    2. Scale analysis (used to validate neglected terms).
  3. State sub-case assumptions explicitly (for example, high-$Pr$ and low-$Pr$ limits).

💡 For constant properties and forced convection, velocity can often be solved first and then used in the energy equation. In free convection, momentum and energy equations are coupled and should be solved together.

  1. Momentum equation stage (e.g., Blasius-type similarity solution).
    1. Non-dimensionalize governing equations and boundary conditions.
    2. Select an appropriate similarity variable.
    3. Derive and solve the reduced ODE system.
  2. Energy equation stage (e.g., Pohlhausen-type treatment).
    1. Non-dimensionalize governing equations and boundary conditions.
    2. Use the velocity profile from the momentum solution.
    3. Solve the reduced temperature equation.
