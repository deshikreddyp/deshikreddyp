Pressure Poisson Equation

The Pressure Poisson Equation (PPE) is a powerful mathematical tool used in CFD to enforce the incompressibility constraint ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$) in pressure-based solvers.

The derivation begins with the Navier-Stokes momentum equation for an incompressible fluid:

$$\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} = -\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{f}$$

Taking the divergence ($\nabla \cdot$) of the entire equation:

$$\nabla \cdot \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} \right) + \nabla \cdot ((\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u}) = \nabla \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla p \right) + \nabla \cdot (\nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}) + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{f}$$

Using the properties of the divergence operator and the incompressibility constraint:

  1. $\nabla \cdot (\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t}) = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}) = 0$
  2. $\nabla \cdot (\nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}) = \nu \nabla^2 (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}) = 0$

Substituting these into the equation, we arrive at the Pressure Poisson Equation:

$$\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla^2 p = \nabla \cdot [\mathbf{f} - (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}]$$

In many CFD codes (like OpenFOAM), the convective term is often expressed using the identity $\nabla \cdot (\mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}) = (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{u}(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u})$, which simplifies to $\nabla \cdot (\mathbf{u}\mathbf{u})$ for incompressible flows.

Mathematical Foundation: Pressure as a Lagrange Multiplier

The pressure field $p$ can be interpreted as a Lagrange multiplier that enforces the divergence-free constraint on the velocity field.

1. Stokes Flow

For Stokes flow ($\rho \to 0$), the equation is the Euler-Lagrange equation for the minimization of the dissipation functional:

$$\min_{\mathbf{u}} \left( \frac{1}{2} \int_\Omega \mu |\nabla \mathbf{u}|^2 \, d\Omega - \int_\Omega \mathbf{f} \cdot \mathbf{u} \, d\Omega \right)$$

subject to $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$. The pressure $p$ arises as the multiplier for this constraint.

2. Euler Equations

In Arnold's interpretation, the Euler equations describe a geodesic flow on the group of volume-preserving diffeomorphisms. The pressure gradient $\nabla p$ is the force required to keep the particle paths on this manifold (i.e., to keep the flow incompressible).

💡 Note: In numerical implementations like the SIMPLE or PISO algorithms, a "pressure correction" equation is solved, which is a discretized form of the PPE.
