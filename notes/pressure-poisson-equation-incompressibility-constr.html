Pressure Poisson Equation

The Pressure Poisson Equation (PPE) is a mathematical tool used in CFD to enforce the incompressibility constraint ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$) in pressure-based solvers.

The derivation begins with the Navier-Stokes momentum equation for an incompressible fluid:

$$\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} = -\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{f}$$

Taking the divergence ($\nabla \cdot$) of the entire equation:

$$\nabla \cdot \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} \right) + \nabla \cdot ((\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u}) = \nabla \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla p \right) + \nabla \cdot (\nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}) + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{f}$$

Using incompressibility and constant viscosity:

  1. $\nabla \cdot \left(\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t}\right) = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}) = 0$
  2. $\nabla \cdot (\nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}) = \nu \nabla^2 (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}) = 0$

So the PPE is:

$$\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla^2 p = \nabla \cdot [\mathbf{f} - (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}]$$

In many CFD codes (e.g., OpenFOAM), the convective term is also written as $\nabla \cdot (\mathbf{u}\mathbf{u})$ using $\nabla \cdot (\mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}) = (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{u}(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u})$.

Pressure as a Lagrange Multiplier

The pressure field $p$ can be interpreted as a Lagrange multiplier that enforces the divergence-free constraint on velocity.

1. Stokes Flow

For creeping-flow conditions ($Re \ll 1$), inertial terms are negligible and the Stokes equations are recovered. In variational form, pressure appears as the multiplier associated with $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}=0$.

$$\min_{\mathbf{u}} \left( \frac{1}{2} \int_\Omega \mu |\nabla \mathbf{u}|^2 \, d\Omega - \int_\Omega \mathbf{f} \cdot \mathbf{u} \, d\Omega \right)$$

subject to $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$.

2. Euler Equations

In Arnold's interpretation, the Euler equations describe geodesic flow on the manifold of volume-preserving diffeomorphisms. The pressure gradient $\nabla p$ is the force that keeps trajectories on this incompressible manifold.

💡 Note: In numerical implementations like SIMPLE or PISO, one solves a pressure-correction equation, which is a discretized form of the PPE.
