Pressure Poisson Equation

Category: CFD · Math · Algorithms · Navier-Stokes

Last updated: ...

The Pressure Poisson Equation (PPE) is the mathematical mechanism used in CFD to enforce the incompressibility constraint ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$) in pressure-based solvers.

Derivation

Starting with the incompressible Navier-Stokes momentum equation:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} = -\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{f} \]

Taking the divergence ($\nabla \cdot$) of the entire equation and applying $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$, the time-derivative and viscous terms vanish, leaving:

\[ \frac{1}{\rho}\nabla^2 p = \nabla \cdot [\mathbf{f} - (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}] \]
Numerical Implementation.

In algorithms like SIMPLE or PISO, a Pressure Correction equation is solved instead of the full PPE. This correction equation is essentially a discretized Poisson problem where the source term is the divergence of the intermediate velocity field.

Theoretical Interpretation

The pressure field can be viewed through several mathematical lenses:

1. Lagrange Multiplier

In variational terms, pressure is the Lagrange multiplier that constrains the velocity field to the manifold of divergence-free functions. In Stokes flow:

\[ \inf_{\mathbf{u}} \sup_{p} \left( \frac{1}{2} \int_\Omega \mu |\nabla \mathbf{u}|^2 \, d\Omega - \int_\Omega \mathbf{f} \cdot \mathbf{u} \, d\Omega - \int_\Omega p (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}) \, d\Omega \right) \]

2. Geodesic Projection

According to Arnold's geometric interpretation, the pressure gradient is the force required to project the advective velocity onto the space of volume-preserving transformations.

Boundary Conditions for PPE.

Calculating proper BCs for the Poisson equation is subtle. Often, a Neumann condition $\frac{\partial p}{\partial n}$ is derived by projecting the momentum equation onto the boundary normal.
