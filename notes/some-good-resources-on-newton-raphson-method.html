Newton-Raphson Method Resources

Category: Numerical Methods · Nonlinear Solvers · Newton-Raphson · FEM

Last updated: ...

The Newton-Raphson method is a powerful technique for finding the roots of nonlinear systems of equations $F(u) = 0$. In computational mechanics, it is the standard approach for solving nonlinear finite element problems.

The Algorithm

Given an initial guess $u_0$, the solution is updated iteratively:

\[ u_{k+1} = u_k - [J(u_k)]^{-1} F(u_k) \]

where $J(u_k) = \frac{\partial F}{\partial u}$ is the Jacobian matrix (or Tangent Stiffness Matrix in structural mechanics).

Curated Resources

  • Nonlinear Finite Elements (Wikiversity): A specialized overview focusing on discretized mechanics.
  • ETH Zurich - FEM II Lecture Notes: Includes rigorous derivations of the tangent stiffness matrix and line-search globalization.
Convergence.

Newton-Raphson exhibits quadratic convergence near the root, meaning the number of correct digits roughly doubles each iteration. However, success depends heavily on a good initial guess.

Practical Tip.

For highly nonlinear problems (like buckling or complex contact), standard Newton often fails. Incremental Arc-Length methods or Continuation methods are used to traverse the equilibrium path.
