PETSc GAMG Preconditioner

Category: PETSc · Algorithms · HPC · Multigrid

Last updated: ...

Algebraic Multigrid (AMG) is an essential tool for solving large-scale linear systems. PETSc's GAMG (Geometric-Algebraic Multigrid) is a powerful implementation that builds hierarchies based on the matrix graph, making it ideal for monolithic CFD and FSI solvers.

Why AMG?

Classical smoothers (like Jacobi) are excellent at removing high-frequency error but stall on low-frequency error. Multigrid shifts the slow error to coarser grids where it becomes easier to remove.

How GAMG Works

  1. Graph Construction: Builds a graph from the sparse matrix connectivity.
  2. Aggregation: Groups unknowns into "aggregates" to form coarser levels.
  3. Galerkin Product: Builds coarse operators recursively: $A_c = R A_f P$.
  4. Cycle Application: Uses V-cycles or W-cycles to propagate error corrections across levels.

Optimal Monolithic GAMG Settings

For a monolithic FSI solver on 256 ranks, the following CustomSolver configuration is a robust starting point:

petsc_options={
    "pc_gamg_type": "agg",
    "pc_gamg_repartition": "true",
    "pc_gamg_process_eq_limit": "200",
    "pc_gamg_coarse_eq_limit": "1000",
    "pc_mg_cycle_type": "v",
    "mg_levels_ksp_type": "richardson",
    "mg_levels_pc_type": "jacobi",
    "mg_coarse_pc_type": "lu"
}
Parallel Efficiency.

At high rank counts (e.g., 256), `pc_gamg_repartition` is critical to avoid load imbalances on coarse levels where the number of active processes decreases.

Tuning Strategy

  • Outer Solver: Use fgmres if the multigrid cycle is variable or nonlinear.
  • Hierarchy Construction: Adjust pc_gamg_threshold to filter weak couplings (but be careful in mixed systems).
  • Coarse Grid: Keep the coarsest grid small enough for lu to be efficient but large enough to capture global modes.
Structural Aids.

GAMG works best when provided with MatSetBlockSize() or a near-null space. These are not just tuning knobs but fundamental algebraic inputs.

Summary

  1. Monolithic gamg often beats FieldSplit for complex Jacobian couplings.
  2. Coarse-grid parallelism is the most important tuning area on large clusters.
  3. Always monitor ksp_monitor_true_residual to verify the stability of the hierarchy.
