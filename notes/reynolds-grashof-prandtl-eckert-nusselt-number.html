Dimensionless Numbers in Heat Transfer

Category: Physics · CFD · Fluid Mechanics · Scaling

Last updated: ...

Dimensionless numbers are key parameters that govern fluid flow and heat transfer. They arise naturally during the non-dimensionalization of governing equations and provide a way to compare the relative importance of competing physical effects.

Reynolds Number ($Re$).

The ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces. It characterizes the flow regime:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho u L}{\mu} = \frac{u L}{\nu} \]

Primary indicator for the transition from laminar to turbulent flow.

Nusselt Number ($Nu$).

The ratio of convective to conductive heat transfer across a boundary:

\[ Nu = \frac{h L}{k} \]

Represents the enhancement of heat transfer due to fluid motion.

Prandtl Number ($Pr$).

The ratio of momentum diffusivity to thermal diffusivity:

\[ Pr = \frac{\nu}{\alpha} = \frac{\mu C_p}{k} \]

A fluid property that determines the relative thickness of velocity and thermal boundary layers.

Grashof Number ($Gr$).

The ratio of buoyancy to viscous forces acting on a fluid:

\[ Gr = \frac{g \beta (T_s - T_\infty) L^3}{\nu^2} \]

The dimensionless parameter for natural convection, analogous to the Reynolds number for forced convection.

Eckert Number ($Ec$).

Relates the flow's kinetic energy to the enthalpy difference:

\[ Ec = \frac{u^2}{C_p \Delta T} \]

Significant in high-speed flows where viscous dissipation (heating) cannot be neglected.

Rayleigh Number ($Ra$).

Often used in natural convection, defined as $Ra = Gr \cdot Pr$. It determines the onset of convection in a fluid layer heated from below (Rayleigh-Bénard convection).
