Reynolds, Grashof, Prandtl, Eckert, Nusselt Numbers

Dimensionless numbers are key parameters that govern fluid flow and heat transfer. They arise naturally during non-dimensionalization of the governing equations and provide a way to compare the relative importance of different physical effects.

Reynolds Number ($Re$)

The ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces. It characterizes the flow regime (laminar vs. turbulent).

$$Re = \frac{\rho u L}{\mu} = \frac{u L}{\nu}$$

Grashof Number ($Gr$)

Approximates the ratio of buoyancy to viscous forces acting on a fluid. It is the analog of the Reynolds number for natural convection.

$$Gr = \frac{g \beta (T_s - T_\infty) L^3}{\nu^2}$$

Prandtl Number ($Pr$)

The ratio of momentum diffusivity (kinematic viscosity) to thermal diffusivity. It is a fluid property that characterizes the relative thickness of velocity and thermal boundary layers.

$$Pr = \frac{\nu}{\alpha} = \frac{C_p \mu}{k}$$

Eckert Number ($Ec$)

Relates the flow's kinetic energy to the available thermal energy difference. It is used to characterize viscous dissipation effects in high-speed flows.

$$Ec = \frac{u^2}{C_p \Delta T}$$

Nusselt Number ($Nu$)

The ratio of convective to conductive heat transfer across a boundary. It provides a measure of heat-transfer enhancement due to flow.

$$Nu = \frac{h L}{k}$$
