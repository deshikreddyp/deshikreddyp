What is Boussinesq Approximation?

Category: Computational Modeling (Numerical Methods)

Last updated: ...

The Boussinesq Approximation is a simplified model used for natural convection flows where density variations are small. The core idea is that density changes can be neglected everywhere except in the buoyancy term of the momentum equation.

Core Assumptions.
  • Density is treated as constant ($\rho_0$) in the continuity equation and the inertia terms.
  • Density varies linearly with temperature only in the gravity term: \[ \rho \approx \rho_0 [1 - \beta (T - T_0)] \]
  • $\beta$ is the thermal expansion coefficient.

Modified Momentum Equation

Substituting the linear density relation into the Navier-Stokes momentum equation yields:

\[ \rho_0 \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} \right) = -\nabla (p + \rho_0 g z) + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} - \rho_0 \beta (T - T_0) \mathbf{g} \]
Numerical Advantage.

This approximation allows us to solve a system that is mathematically "incompressible" (divergence-free velocity) while still capturing the driving physics of buoyancy-driven flows. This is much more stable than solving the full compressible equations for low-speed natural convection.

Validity.

The approximation is valid when the temperature differences are small enough such that $\beta \Delta T \ll 1$. For gases, this typically holds if the temperature variation is less than 10% of the absolute temperature.
