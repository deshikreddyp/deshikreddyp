What is Boussinesq Approximation?

The Boussinesq Approximation is a simplified model used for natural convection flows where density variations are small. The core assumption is that density changes are neglected except in the buoyancy term of the momentum equation.

Core Assumptions

  • Density is treated as constant ($\rho_0$) in the continuity equation and the inertia terms.
  • Density is allowed to vary with temperature only in the gravity (buoyancy) term.
  • The relationship between density and temperature is assumed to be linear:
$$\rho \approx \rho_0 [1 - \beta (T - T_0)]$$

where $\beta$ is the thermal expansion coefficient, $T_0$ is the reference temperature, and $\rho_0$ is the density at $T_0$.

Modified Momentum Equation

Substituting this into the Navier-Stokes momentum equation:

$$\rho_0 \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} \right) = -\nabla (p + \rho_0 g z) + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} - \rho_0 \beta (T - T_0) \mathbf{g}$$

This approximation significantly simplifies the numerical solution as it avoids solving a fully compressible system of equations while still capturing the physics of buoyancy-driven flows.
