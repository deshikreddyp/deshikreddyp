Thoughts on solving system of linear equations

Choosing the right solver for a system of linear equations $Ax=b$ is a critical decision in computational mechanics. This note summarizes the trade-offs between direct solvers and iterative solvers, specifically for Finite Element (FEM) discretizations like the Laplace operator.

Direct Solvers

Examples: MUMPS, SuperLU_DIST, UMFPACK.

  • Complexity: $O(n^{3/2})$ in 2D and $O(n^2)$ in 3D.
  • Robustness: Will find a solution unless the system is singular.
  • Precision: Computes solutions to machine precision.
  • Speed: Very fast for small to medium problems ($n < 10^5$).

Iterative Solvers (Krylov Methods)

Examples: Conjugate Gradient (CG), GMRES, BiCGSTAB (usually paired with Algebraic Multigrid - AMG).

  • Complexity: Ideally $O(n)$ with good preconditioning.
  • Trade-offs: Designing an effective preconditioner is often the hardest part. Connectivity/mesh quality significantly impacts convergence.
  • Precision: The solution is an approximation; you stop when the residual is below a certain tolerance.
💡
For large-scale simulations ($n \geq 10^9$), scaling is everything. Direct solvers become impossible due to $O(n^2)$ memory and time growth. Iterative methods are the only path forward.
