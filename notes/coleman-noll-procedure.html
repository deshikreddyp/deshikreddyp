Coleman-Noll Procedure

Category: Continuum Mechanics · Thermodynamics · Constitutive Theory

The Coleman-Noll procedure is a systematic method for deriving constitutive relations that are guaranteed to be consistent with the Second Law of Thermodynamics, specifically the Clausius-Duhem inequality.

Core Axioms

The procedure relies on the "Entropy Principle": constitutive equations must satisfy the entropy inequality for all possible thermodynamic processes compatible with the balance laws.

The Clausius-Duhem Inequality.

In its local form, the inequality states: \[ -\rho(\dot{\psi} + \eta \dot{T}) + \mathbf{T} : \mathbf{D} - \frac{1}{T} \mathbf{q} \cdot \nabla T \ge 0 \] where $\psi$ is Helmholtz free energy, $\eta$ is entropy, and $\mathbf{T}$ is the Cauchy stress tensor.

Constitutive Constraints.

By treating the chosen state variables (e.g., deformation gradient, temperature) as arbitrary fields, the Coleman-Noll procedure allows us to "read off" the thermodynamic restrictions on the stress, entropy, and heat flux functions.
