Behind CFD

Category: CFD · Algorithms · Math · Pressure-Velocity

Last updated: ...

The Fundamental Challenge.

The mathematical complexity of computational fluid dynamics (CFD) stems primarily from two sources:

  1. Non-linearity: The convective term $(\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}$ in the momentum equation is non-linear.
  2. Pressure-Velocity Coupling: There is no explicit transport equation for pressure in the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations.

The Role of Pressure

The pressure gradient acts as a source term in the momentum equation. While solving for velocity is straightforward if the pressure field is known, the actual challenge is finding a pressure field that simultaneously satisfies both momentum and continuity ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$).

Implicit Link.

When the correct pressure field is used in the momentum equation, the resulting velocity field automatically satisfies the continuity constraint.

Algorithms: SIMPLE & PISO

These are "pressure-based" algorithms that solve this coupling iteratively:

The SIMPLE Algorithm

  1. Predictor Step: Solve discretized momentum equations with an estimated pressure field to get an intermediate velocity field $\mathbf{u}^*$.
  2. Pressure Poisson: Derive and solve an equation for pressure (or pressure correction) based on the continuity equation.
  3. Corrector Step: Correct the velocity field using the new pressure field: \[ \mathbf{u} = \mathbf{u}^* - A^{-1} \nabla p' \]
  4. Iterate: Repeat until the residuals of both momentum and continuity are below the tolerance.

Mathematical Framework: Matrix Decomposition

In discretized form (FVM or FEM), the momentum system is often expressed as:

\[ \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U} = -\nabla p \]

where $\mathbf{M}$ is the coefficient matrix. Solving this requires decomposing the matrix into diagonal ($\mathbf{A}$) and off-diagonal ($\mathbf{H}$) parts to facilitate the pressure-correction step: \[ \mathbf{A} \mathbf{U} = \mathbf{H} - \nabla p \] leading to the correction form: \[ \mathbf{U} = \mathbf{A}^{-1}\mathbf{H} - \mathbf{A}^{-1}\nabla p \]

Checkerboard Problem.

On collocated grids, a simple central difference for the pressure gradient can lead to unphysical pressure oscillations. This is resolved using Rhie-Chow interpolation, which adds a fourth-order smoothing term to the face flux calculation.
