Numerical Solution of Parbolic and Elliptic Differential Equations

This page discusses the numerical treatment of parabolic (e.g., Heat Equation) and elliptic (e.g., Laplace Equation) PDEs using Finite Difference and Finite Element methods.

Key Concepts

  • Parabolic PDEs: Characterize dissipative processes. Information travels infinitely fast but decays over time. Typical solver: Implicit/Explicit Time Marching.
  • Elliptic PDEs: Characterize steady-state processes. Information propagates throughout the domain instantly. Typical solver: Sparse direct or iterative linear solvers (GMRES, Conjugate Gradient).
💡
Historical reference on numerical solutions: A.R. Mitchell (1969).
