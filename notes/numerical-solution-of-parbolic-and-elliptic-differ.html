Numerical Solution of Parabolic & Elliptic PDEs

Category: Numerical Methods · Heat Equation · Laplace Equation · FDM/FEM

Last updated: ...

The numerical treatment of parabolic (e.g., Heat Equation) and elliptic (e.g., Laplace Equation) PDEs typically involves systematic spatial discretization using Finite Difference (FDM) or Finite Element (FEM) methods.

Key Concepts

  • Parabolic PDEs: Characterize dissipative processes. Information travels infinitely fast but decays over time. Typical solver: Implicit/Explicit Time Marching.
  • Elliptic PDEs: Characterize steady-state processes. Information propagates throughout the domain instantly. Typical solver: Sparse direct or iterative linear solvers (GMRES, Conjugate Gradient).
Historical Reference.

A classic resource on numerical techniques for these equations is A.R. Mitchell (1969), which covers the transition from FDM to early FEM concepts.

Algorithmic Focus.

Stability analysis (Von Neumann) and consistency checks are paramount for parabolic marchers, whereas preconditioning is the bottleneck for elliptic solvers.
