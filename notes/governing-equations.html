Governing Equations

Material Derivative

The material derivative (also known as the substantive or Lagrangian derivative) describes the time rate of change of a physical quantity for a fluid particle moving with the flow velocity $\mathbf{u}$.

$$\frac{D\phi}{Dt} = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\phi$$

Reynolds Transport Theorem (RTT)

RTT provides a link between the rate of change of a property in a control volume (Eulerian) and the rate of change for a fixed group of particles (Lagrangian).

$$\frac{d}{dt}\int_{\Omega(t)} f d\Omega = \int_{\Omega(t)} \frac{\partial f}{\partial t} d\Omega + \int_{\Gamma(t)} f (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{n}) d\Gamma$$

Gauss Divergence Theorem

Relates the volume integral of the divergence of a vector field to the surface integral of the field over the boundary.

$$\int_{\Omega} \nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} d\Omega = \int_{\Gamma} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} d\Gamma$$

Continuity Equation

Representing the conservation of mass. For an incompressible fluid, the density is constant, leading to the zero-divergence condition:

$$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$$

Momentum Equation

Derived from Newton's Second Law ($\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$). For an incompressible Newtonian fluid, the Navier-Stokes momentum equation is:

$$\rho \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} \right) = -\nabla p + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{f}$$
