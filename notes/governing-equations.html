Governing Equations

Category: Fluid Mechanics · Navier-Stokes · Conservation Laws

Last updated: ...

Material Derivative

The material derivative (substantive or Lagrangian derivative) describes the time rate of change of a physical quantity $\phi$ for a fluid particle moving with the flow velocity $\mathbf{u}$.

\[ \frac{D\phi}{Dt} = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\phi \]

Reynolds Transport Theorem (RTT)

RTT provides the link between the rate of change of a property for a fixed group of particles (Lagrangian) and the rate of change within a control volume (Eulerian).

\[ \frac{d}{dt}\int_{\Omega(t)} f \, d\Omega = \int_{\Omega(t)} \frac{\partial f}{\partial t} \, d\Omega + \int_{\Gamma(t)} f (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{n}) \, d\Gamma \]
Gauss Divergence Theorem.

This theorem is fundamental for converting volume integrals of divergences into surface fluxes: \[ \int_{\Omega} \nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} \, d\Omega = \int_{\Gamma} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} \, d\Gamma \]

Continuity Equation

Representing the conservation of mass. For an incompressible fluid, density $\rho$ is constant, leading to the solenoidal condition:

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0 \]

Momentum Equation

Derived from Newton’s Second Law ($\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$). For an incompressible Newtonian fluid, the Navier-Stokes momentum equation is:

\[ \rho \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} \right) = -\nabla p + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{f} \]
Conservative vs. Non-Conservative Form.

The choice of form depends on the numerical method. Finite Volume Methods typically solve the conservative form to ensure strict conservation of properties across cell faces.
