Velocity Potential

Category: Computational Modeling (Numerical Methods)

Last updated: ...

In fluid dynamics, the Velocity Potential ($\phi$) is a scalar function used to describe irrotational flow. When a flow is irrotational, the velocity field $\mathbf{u}$ can be expressed as the gradient of this potential.

Mathematical Definition.

For an irrotational flow, the vorticity $\omega = \nabla \times \mathbf{u} = 0$. This vector identity allows us to define $\phi$ such that:

\[ \mathbf{u} = \nabla \phi \]

Laplace’s Equation

For an incompressible ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$) and irrotational flow, substituting the potential into the continuity equation leads to Laplace's equation:

\[ \nabla^2 \phi = 0 \]

This is the foundation of Potential Flow Theory, where complex flow fields can be constructed by superposition of simple linear solutions (sources, sinks, vortices).

Bernoulli for Potential Flow.

For unsteady, irrotational, incompressible flow, the Bernoulli equation integrates to: \[ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2}|\nabla \phi|^2 + \frac{p}{\rho} + gz = f(t) \]

Irrotationality.

Velocity potential only exists if the flow is irrotational. In regions with significant vorticity (like boundary layers or wake regions), the theory breaks down and the full Navier-Stokes equations must be used.
