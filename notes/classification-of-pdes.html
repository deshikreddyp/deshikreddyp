Classification of PDEs

To solve PDEs, you need to get the feeling of PDEs. Obviously one must know how to classify them into linear-nonlinear, homogeneous and non-homogeneous. However, there is a richer way to classify PDEs based on the characteristics.

Nonlinear PDEs are usually solved by linearizing them and Newton Raphson methods. Hence, nonlinear PDEs can be solved by one additional step in computations. Homogeneous and nonhomogeneous are not that different - so we forget the classification.

Here we deal with linear PDEs of order II, in which most of the PDEs fall under. Before that lets introduce a small concept in physical sense, - Boundary value problems, Time evolution problems. And a PDE can be mix of both as well. The reason we look time as a different quantity in PDEs is all due to its physically one-way-one-dimensional nature. Time can only march forward.
