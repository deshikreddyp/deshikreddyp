Pressure

Category: Fluid Mechanics · Pressure · Navier-Stokes · Thermodynamics

Last updated: ...

Pressure is a macroscopically defined quantity representing the normal force exerted by a fluid per unit area. In fluid mechanics, it is a crucial variable for determining flow behavior and structural loading.

Types of Pressure

  • Static Pressure: The actual thermodynamic pressure of the fluid.
  • Dynamic Pressure: The pressure associated with fluid motion, defined as $\frac{1}{2}\rho u^2$.
  • Total (Stagnation) Pressure: The sum of static and dynamic pressure.
Hydrostatic Pressure.

In a fluid at rest under gravity, the pressure increases with depth according to: \[ p = p_0 + \rho g h \] where $h$ is the depth below the reference level.

Absolute vs. Gauge Pressure.

Absolute Pressure is measured relative to a perfect vacuum, while Gauge Pressure is measured relative to the local atmospheric pressure ($p_{gauge} = p_{abs} - p_{atm}$).

Incompressible Flow

For incompressible flows, pressure is not an independent thermodynamic variable. Instead, it is a mathematical field that instantly adjusts its value to satisfy the continuity equation ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$). This is the reason why pressure changes propagate at infinite speed in incompressible models.
