Flow Boundary Conditions

Category: Boundary Conditions

Last updated: ...

This page discusses the application of flow boundary conditions in computational domains like pipe flow or arterial geometry, where accurate prescription at inlets and outlets is critical for physical validity.

Inlet Conditions

Usually, the inlet condition is known. A Dirichlet boundary condition is commonly employed, requiring both temporal and spatial data. A parabolic velocity profile combined with time-varying waveforms is a standard choice for laminar pipe flow.

Outlet Conditions

At the outlet, one can prescribe either velocity or pressure. However, determining the specific flow profile at the outlet can be challenging as it technically lies "outside" the controlled computational domain.

Popular Model Setups (Liu et al. 2015).
  • V-NS0: Inlet velocity profile; zero normal stress at outlet.
  • V-P: Inlet velocity profile; time-dependent pressure at outlet.
  • P-P: Time-dependent pressure at both inlet and outlet.
Key Conclusions.
  • Inlet Robustness: Imposing a velocity waveform at the inlet is generally more stable than using pressure conditions.
  • Phasic Influence: The velocity waveform exerts a stronger influence on the phasic shape of the pressure distribution than the pressure BC itself.
  • Sensitivity: P-P models are highly sensitive to the accuracy of outlet pressure data, making V-P setups preferred for patient-specific simulations.

Advanced Outflow Management

Regular traction-zero conditions may produce Realistic results if flow reversal (backflow) occurs at the boundary. The traction vector $\mathbf{t}$ on the boundary is defined by the stress tensor:

\[ \mathbf{t} = \mu \left( \nabla \mathbf{u} + (\nabla \mathbf{u})^T \right) \cdot \mathbf{n} - p \mathbf{n} \]
Backflow Stabilization.

For truncated domains like arterial bifurcations, a velocity-dependent traction condition is often used to prevent numerical divergence during flow reversal: \[ \mathbf{t} = \beta \rho (\mathbf{v} \otimes \mathbf{v}) \cdot \mathbf{n} \]
