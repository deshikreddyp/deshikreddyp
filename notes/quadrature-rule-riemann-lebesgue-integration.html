Quadrature Rule, Riemann & Lebesgue Integration

Quadrature

When we are unable to solve analytical solution of a problem, whether it is due to complexity in geometry or equation, we use numerical methods, which is an approximate solution. And we always strive to make the numerical solution accurate.

Derivatives and Integrals are the basic units of any PDE, thus we need to ultimately make it simpler, whether it may be linear or ODE or solvable PDEs. So, let us develop Numerical Derivatives and Numerical Integrations.,

You may also have known about numerical derivatives., (FINITE DIFFERENCES). Very similarly it is also important to know about how integration is calculated/approximated. This is how computers solves stuff.

In numerical analysis, a quadrature rule is an approximation of the definite integral of a function, usually stated as weighted sum of function values at specified point within the domain of integration.
