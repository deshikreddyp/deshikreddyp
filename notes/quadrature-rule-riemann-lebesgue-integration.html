Quadrature, Riemann & Lebesgue Integration

Quadrature Rule

When an analytical solution to an integral is unavailable due to complex geometry or equations, we use numerical quadrature. A quadrature rule approximates a definite integral as a weighted sum of function values at specific points (nodes).

$$\int_{-1}^{1} f(x) \, dx \approx \sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i f(x_i)$$

This is the fundamental way computers solve integral equations in methods like Finite Elements.

Integration Theory

Beyond numerical approximation, it is important to distinguish between different theoretical frameworks of integration:

  • Riemann Integration: The standard approach taught in calculus, based on summing the areas of vertical rectangles.
  • Lebesgue Integration: A more powerful theory based on measure theory, which can integrate a broader class of functions (e.g., highly discontinuous functions).
